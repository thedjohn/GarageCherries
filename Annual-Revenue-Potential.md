# GarageCherries — Annual Revenue Potential

*Analysis as of June 2026 | Projections cover Year 1 (startup), Year 2 (growth), Year 3 (scale)*

---

## Market Context

The collector and classic car market generates an estimated **$50–70 billion** in annual transactions in North America. Bring a Trailer (BAT) alone processed over $1.4 billion in sales in 2023, taking a 5% buyer's premium. Hemmings, Cars & Bids, Classic.com, and AutoHunter all operate profitably in this niche. The key differentiator for GarageCherries is the **dealer-subscription SaaS model** layered with performance marketing, which creates predictable recurring revenue independent of transaction volume.

There are an estimated **8,000–12,000** active classic/collector car dealers and brokers in the United States. Even capturing 1% of that market (80–120 dealers) at modest subscription rates produces meaningful recurring revenue.

## Priority Unlock Roadmap

The gap between Year 1 and Year 2 revenue is primarily closed by three actions:

1. **Wire Stripe** *(unlocks ~$250k/year by Year 2)*
   — Dealer subscriptions, private seller listings, featured upgrades, and homepage spotlight all require payment processing. This is the single highest-leverage engineering task on the roadmap.

2. **Launch auctions with 5 curated cars** *(unlocks ~$79k in Year 2)*
   — Even at low volume, auction fees significantly outperform subscription revenue per transaction. A curated white-glove model (GarageCherries selects and photographs the cars) commands higher premiums.

3. **Add financing and insurance widgets to listing pages** *(unlocks ~$38k by Year 2)*
   — Both Hagerty and J.J. Best Banc have affiliate programs. A single API integration per partner adds passive revenue to every transaction on the platform.

## Key Assumptions & Risks

| Assumption | Risk if Wrong |
|---|---|
| 26 paying dealers by end of Year 1 | Harder to recruit without existing inventory. Solve with free trial + white-glove onboarding for the first 10. |
| Traffic grows to 250k pages/month by Year 2 | Requires consistent SEO investment. Encyclopedia and guides are the primary content engine for organic search. |
| Auction model launches in Year 2 | Requires significant trust-building with sellers before consignment. May slip to Year 3. |
| Affiliate partner approvals | Hagerty and Woodside have application processes. Apply now; approval takes 30–90 days. |
| Churn stays below 8%/month | Churn above 12% collapses the subscription model. Focus on dealer success metrics (inquiries, views, sales). |
